Hive types overview

An introduction to the main hive designs used in modern beekeeping, and how to choose one that fits your beekeeping style.

National hive (UK standard)

  • Square box design with sloping sides.
  • Common in the UK; parts widely available and interchangeable.
  • Uses 14×12 or standard deep brood boxes; easy to handle and inspect.

Best balance for beginners: compact, manageable, easy to source spares.

Langstroth

  • Worldwide standard; straight-sided boxes stacked vertically.
  • Commercial favourite; suits migratory or large-scale setups.
  • Deep, medium, and shallow box formats for flexible brood/honey space.

Warre (the “people’s hive”)

  • Top-bar design where new boxes are added underneath (“nadiring”).
  • Less frequent inspections; mimics natural cavity expansion.
  • Favoured by low-intervention or natural beekeepers.

Top-bar hives

  • Horizontal layout with individual bars rather than frames.
  • Good for demonstration and education—shows full comb building.
  • Not easily compatible with extractor equipment or standard frames.

Flow hive

  • Modified Langstroth-style box with plastic flow frames for honey extraction without uncapping.
  • Convenient for hobbyists; requires attentive colony management to avoid neglect.
  • Ensure good ventilation and monitor swarming as brood space is limited.

Choosing your hive

  • Match your back strength — Nationals are lighter than Langstroths.
  • Think about availability of spares and frames locally.
  • Consider your management style: hands-on inspections vs. low-intervention approaches.
  • Standardisation matters if you’ll run multiple hives or join a club apiary.
