Apiary layout basics

Positioning hives for safety, efficiency, and healthy bee flight lines in any setting.

Choosing a site

  • Sunlight: morning sun warms the entrance early; partial afternoon shade prevents overheating.
  • Shelter: hedge, fence, or trees to break cold winds but not block airflow.
  • Access: flat ground, year-round entry for lifting boxes safely.
  • Water: clean nearby source prevents bees crowding neighbours’ ponds.

Hive orientation

  • Entrances ideally face south-east for early warmth.
  • Vary facing angles slightly between hives to reduce drifting.
  • Keep clear flight paths (2–3 m in front) free of tall obstacles.

Spacing & layout

  • Between hives: ~1 m minimum, 2–3 m ideal.
  • Rows: stagger so entrances don’t align directly opposite each other.
  • Label or colour-code boxes to help bees orient by visual cues.

Urban and garden settings

  • Keep entrances above head height or behind screens to lift flight lines.
  • Maintain considerate distances from paths, neighbours, and boundaries.
  • Provide shallow water source (pebbles in trays) during dry spells.

Check local council or landlord permissions before placing hives on rooftops or allotments.

Rural and out-apiary setups

  • Ensure solid stands: paving slabs, bricks, or treated timber blocks.
  • Protect from livestock with fencing or rails.
  • Watch for damp hollows—hives need dry feet.

Workflow & safety

  • Leave at least one clear side for inspections (ideally the back).
  • Keep smoker, tools, and spare supers on a portable stand within easy reach.
  • Mark an emergency retreat path free of clutter or trip hazards.
