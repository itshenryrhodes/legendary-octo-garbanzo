Winter prep checklist

A practical, step-by-step checklist to prepare colonies for winter: stores, health, equipment, ventilation, and emergency plans.

Timing at a glance

  • Late summer: final harvest, monitor mites, treat if indicated.
  • Early autumn: heavy syrup feeding to target winter weight.
  • Late autumn: weatherproofing, entrance reducers, mouse guards.
  • Mid-winter: quick checks only; emergency fondant if needed.

Stores & target weights

  • Heft each hive; aim for robust stores before cold sets in.
  • Guides: National ~20â€“25 kg; Langstroth ~30â€“35 kg of honey/syrup equivalent.
  • If light, feed heavy syrup (2:1 by volume) until target weight is reached.

Stop syrup once consistently cold; switch to fondant for emergencies only.

Health checks

  • Confirm low Varroa counts post-treatment.
  • Inspect brood pattern late autumn; note queen status and temperament.
  • Remove old/black comb and excess burr comb that impedes bee space.

Hardware & weatherproofing

  • Roof: sound, watertight, and weighted against wind.
  • Entrances: fit mouse guards; reduce width to deter robbing and wasps.
  • Stands: level and secure; ensure drainage under hives.
  • Spare kit: dry supers and frames stored moth-safe.

Ventilation & insulation

  • Maintain a small top vent or breathable crown board to prevent condensation.
  • Consider a thin insulation board over the crown board in very cold areas.
  • Leave an upper bee space to avoid squashing clustered bees when checking.

Cold + dry beats warm + damp. Aim to avoid condensation dripping on the winter cluster.

Feeding options

  • Heavy syrup (autumn): rapid feeder or contact feeder above the brood box.
  • Fondant (winter emergency): directly above the cluster under the crown board.
  • Feed in evening; avoid spills; keep entrances reduced.

Records & planning

  • Log weight/heft, mite checks, feed amounts, queen status, and equipment changes.
  • Note colonies needing early spring attention (light, small, requeening candidates).
  • Prepare sugar, fondant, and spare equipment now to avoid mid-winter shortages.
