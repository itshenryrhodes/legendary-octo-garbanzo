Feeding basics

When to feed, what to feed, and how to feed safely without triggering robbing or stressing the colony.

When to feed

  • Spring build-up: light syrup if colonies are light and weather limits forage.
  • After installing a nucleus/split: to draw comb and establish brood nest.
  • After harvest (late summer): heavy syrup to build winter stores quickly.
  • Winter emergency: fondant if stores are dangerously low.

Feeders & placement

  • Contact feeder (jar/bucket with holes): inverted over feed hole; minimal drowning.
  • Miller/rapid feeder: sits over crown board; easy to refill with minimal disturbance.
  • Frame feeder: replaces a frame inside the brood box; useful in poor weather.

How to mix & feed

Mixing syrup

  1. Heat clean water to warm (not boiling). Add sugar gradually while stirring.
  2. Optional pinch of citric acid helps invert sugar slightly; do not overdo.
  3. Cool to room temperature before use.

Troubleshooting

  • Robbing: bees fighting at the entrance; reduce entrance, cover feed holes, and stop external spills.
  • Mouldy syrup: small batches; clean feeders regularly.
  • Bees ignoring feed: flow is on or feeder design is unfamiliar; try a different feeder or move closer to cluster.
