Swarm signs & prevention

Recognise early swarm signals and use simple, reliable actions to keep colonies productive and intact.

Why colonies swarm

  • Population pressure: brood nest congestion and lack of laying space.
  • Queen age/pheromone: declining pheromone distribution in large colonies.
  • Flow timing: strong nectar flows can accelerate crowding and cell building.

Goal: relieve pressure and maintain open brood space during the build-up.

Early signs (act now)

  • Cups with eggs or jelly (â€œchargedâ€ queen cups) on frame bottoms/edges.
  • Backfilling brood with nectar; queen running out of empty cells.
  • Bees hanging as a dense beard outside on warm days.

Inspection cadence

  • In swarm season, inspect every 7 days (maximum).
  • If charged cups present, tighten to every 5â€“6 days.
  • Always note number/position of cells and brood pattern.

Prevention basics

  • Add space early: super before comb is wall-to-wall; give drawn comb if possible.
  • Open the brood nest: intersperse one drawn comb (not foundation) to create laying space.
  • Equalise: move a frame of emerging brood to a weaker colony (disease checks first).

Avoid splitting brood with cold empty frames in poor weather â€” chilling risks brood loss.

When queen cells are present

Artificial swarm (classic)

  1. Move original hive aside; place a new hive on the old stand with drawn comb/foundation.
  2. Find the queen and place her with one frame of brood and all the flying bees in the new hive.
  3. Return remaining brood frames with one good open cell to the original hive (now off the flight line).
  4. After 7 days, knock down any extra cells; keep one best, well-fed cell.

Simple split (nucleus + parent)

  1. Make up a nucleus with 2â€“3 frames of brood, food, and adhering bees.
  2. Ensure either the queen or a good queen cell is included (not both).
  3. Leave the parent colony with eggs/larvae or a single selected queen cell.

Demaree (foragers retained on original stand)

  1. Place all brood above a queen excluder in a second box with one gap of foundation/drawn comb in the lower box for the queen to lay.
  2. Shake bees down, leaving mostly brood above; add supers as needed.
  3. Destroy cells after 7 days; repeat weekly until flow passes.

Quick decision guide

  • No cells, just congestion: add space; open brood nest; recheck in 7 days.
  • Uncharged cups only: space + recheck in 5â€“7 days.
  • Charged cells (jelly/larvae): choose a method (artificial swarm / split / Demaree) now.
  • Multiple sealed cells: assume swarm imminent; act immediately and reduce to a single best cell.
