Varroa monitoring schedule

Season-by-season thresholds and simple methods to spot Varroa early and act in good time.

Why monitor?

Varroa destructor weakens colonies by feeding on developing bees and spreading viruses. Regular, light-touch checks let you time any interventions well and avoid unnecessary treatments.

  • Use one consistent method for comparable results.
  • Record counts with date, weather, brood status.
  • Respond to trends, not one-off spikes.

Common methods

  • Alcohol wash (300 bees): accurate; counts mites per 100 bees.
  • Powdered sugar roll (300 bees): non-lethal alternative; slightly less accurate.
  • Sticky board/natural drop: board under an open mesh floor for 3 days; lower precision but useful trend line.

Pick one as your primary method and sanity-check with another occasionally.

Quick thresholds

  • Alcohol/sugar roll: ~2–3 mites/100 bees in spring warrants closer watch; ≥3–4 in the main season usually triggers action.
  • Natural drop (3-day): persistent daily averages above low single digits outside broodless periods suggest rising pressure.

Use local best practice—thresholds vary by climate, season, and colony strength.

Season-by-season schedule (UK-leaning, adapt locally)

Late winter / very early spring

  • Quick natural-drop check (3 days) during a steady cold spell.
  • If broodless confirmed, note this window—it’s when many beekeepers apply winter treatments.

Spring build-up (March–May)

  • Monthly alcohol/sugar roll on ~300 bees from the brood nest.
  • Track trend. If counts reach ~2–3/100 bees, plan a follow-up check within 2 weeks.
  • Rotate drone comb only if you already use it for management—don’t add complexity if new.

Early–mid summer (June–July)

  • Every 2–3 weeks: roll test + spot natural-drop trend.
  • Approaching ≥3–4/100 bees or fast upward trend → prepare an intervention once honey is off.

Late summer after harvest (July–August)

  • Re-test within a week of taking supers off; treat promptly if over threshold.
  • Re-check efficacy 10–14 days after treatment (same method).

Autumn (September–October)

  • Monthly check to confirm downward trend post-treatment.
  • Ensure colonies enter winter with low mite loads.

Winter (November–January)

  • Look for broodless windows for a single accurate natural-drop or targeted check.
  • Only minimal disturbance; prioritise hive weather-tightness and stores.

Health note: choose approved interventions for your country and follow the label. This page focuses on monitoring; treatment specifics vary by region and product.

Method snapshots

Alcohol wash (most accurate)

  1. Collect ~300 bees (about half a cup) from a brood frame into a ventilated jar.
  2. Add alcohol, shake gently 60 seconds, pour through mesh onto a white tray to count mites.
  3. Calculate mites per 100 bees; record and compare over time.

Powdered sugar roll (non-lethal)

  1. Collect ~300 bees into a jar with mesh lid.
  2. Add 2 tbsp icing sugar, roll to coat, wait 1 minute, shake over white tray; count mites.
  3. Release bees back to the hive.

Natural drop / sticky board

  1. Insert sticky board under open mesh floor for 72 hours.
  2. Count daily average; repeat monthly to track trend.
