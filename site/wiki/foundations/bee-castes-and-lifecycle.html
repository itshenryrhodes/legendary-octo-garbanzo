Bee castes & lifecycle (quick tour)

Meet the three castes—queen, worker, drone—and how eggs become adults across the brood cycle.

Castes at a glance

  • Queen — single fertile female; lays eggs and produces colony pheromones.
  • Workers — sterile females; do nursing, foraging, comb building, guarding.
  • Drones — males; mate with queens; no stinger or foraging duties.

Lifecycle timeline

All start as eggs. Development time varies by caste:

  • Queen: ~16 days (egg→adult)
  • Worker: ~21 days
  • Drone: ~24 days

Brood stages: egg (days 1–3), larva (open brood), pupa (capped), then emergence as an adult.

Why it matters

Recognizing brood stages helps you spot queen events, disease, and nutrition issues early.
