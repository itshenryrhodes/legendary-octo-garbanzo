Antenna

A paired sensory organ on a bee’s head used for smell, taste, touch, temperature, humidity, and vibration detection.

Definition

Antenna (plural: antennae): segmented feeler composed of a basal scape, a jointed pedicel, and a multi-segmented flagellum. Packed with sensory hairs and pits (sensilla) that detect odours, pheromones, CO2, humidity, temperature, and air movement.

Pronunciation: /æn-ten-ə/ • /æn-tiː-niː/

What it’s for

  • Smell & taste: recognising nectar sources, nestmate odours, alarm and queen pheromones.
  • Touch & vibration: close-up navigation on comb; detecting waggle-dance signals.
  • Climate sensing: maintaining brood temperature and humidity.

Notes & tips

  • Damaged antennae can impair foraging and communication.
  • Cool, gentle smoke avoids overwhelming antennal receptors during inspections.
