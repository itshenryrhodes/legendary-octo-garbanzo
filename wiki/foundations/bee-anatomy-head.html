Overview

The honey bee head is a compact toolkit for perception and manipulation. It carries the compound eyes and ocelli for vision, antennae for smell, taste, touch, and humidity sensing, and a versatile set of mouthparts for gathering liquids, sculpting wax, and processing food. Inside, specialized glands and a highly efficient brain integrate sensory input with learned experience.
